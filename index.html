Diagnostic Insights

Feel like you're flying blind? Are the default settings of Google Analytics giving you the blues? Want to understand which users are "good" and keepers versus "bad" and should be ignored? Understanding the basic rhythm and shape of your business metrics is the first step to any engagement. It's very similar to a doctor appointment: there are certain things that get measured and recorded, regardless of your primary reason for the visit. While the health industry looks at ratios like BMI and blood pressure, common ratios a business should monitor may include DAU/MAU ratio, CAC over AARPU or LTV, percent growth per week, net promoter scores, or viral coefficients. Checking your vitals early on will also serve as a benchmark to see if a treatment plan is making your conditions worse or better. A good check-up will sometimes reveal valuable, actionable steps that your business can immediately take to see rapid, dramatic improvement. While we wait on the lab for your initial test results, we'll also check your pulse, and ask how well you sleep at night. You don't mind if we listen to the ol' ticker do you? Predictive Intelligence

Given enough data, it's easier than ever to create and train an intelligent application to predict virtually any future event, and to do so with accuracy that rivals that of a human being. Such an intelligent application can be designed to also receive feedback, so that it learns from incorrect answers, and within microseconds is ready to make its next prediction more accurate. Intelligent agents are becoming common parts of customer support services as chat bots and answering services, shopping cart recommendation features, advertising optimization tools, creating more challenging non-human characters in video games, and predicting where the next fire emergency will happen. In this last example, several cities in the U.S. already use this type of application to be make safety inspections cheaper, better, and faster by more effectively prioritizing which buildings a fire marshal should visit first.

Ranking and Recommendations

Recommendation systems are data-driven applications that give your users features like "Do you know Alice? You have friends in common"; "Customers who had similar shopping cart items also bought Y - add it to your cart?"; "You watched Stargate and liked it; have you watched Farscape?" Ranking and scoring systems help rank, prioritize, score, and filter items, people, and decisions based on a combination of data inputs. The first page of Google search results is one such ranking system. Your credit score is another. Ranking and Recommendation systems are one of the easiest and most successful areas of data science to apply to any sized problem. Adding a recommendation system to your product puts in the same class as Amazon, LinkedIn, Facebook, OKCupid, and Netflix.
